Monster Hunter Wilds Achievement Guide

A clean Monster Hunter Wilds achievement route should separate natural story progress from long-tail hunting, crafting, collection, and cleanup tasks.

Batch 004 Published May 31, 2026
Cover image placeholder

Quick Answer

Play through the main hunt flow first, keep notes on rare materials and optional discoveries, then build a cleanup checklist for weapon upgrades, side activities, large monster goals, and exploration tasks.

Completion Roadmap

  1. Finish core story hunts without forcing every optional task early.
  2. Upgrade one comfortable weapon path before spreading materials too thin.
  3. Track monsters, regions, and materials that appear less often.
  4. Return for collection and crafting cleanup once travel is easier.

Mistakes To Avoid

  • Burning rare materials on weapons you do not plan to use.
  • Ignoring side systems until the final cleanup becomes too large.
  • Repeating hunts without checking which part, carve, or reward you need.
  • Switching weapons constantly before learning one reliable matchup plan.
Checklist placeholder

Best Recommendation

Treat the first playthrough as setup. Build comfort, unlock mobility and crafting options, then use a focused checklist to finish achievement categories without wasting hunts.

FAQ

Can I miss achievements?

Most completion routes are easier if you track progress early, even when cleanup remains possible later.

Should I farm during the story?

Farm enough to stay comfortable, but save heavy material grinding for after your options expand.

What is the best weapon for achievements?

The best weapon is the one you can use consistently across repeated hunts.

When should I start cleanup?

Start after the main progression opens enough systems, regions, and upgrade paths to reduce repeat work.
