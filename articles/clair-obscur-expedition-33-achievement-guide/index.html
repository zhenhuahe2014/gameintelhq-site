Clair Obscur Expedition 33 Achievement Guide

A clean Clair Obscur achievement run is mostly about planning before the late-game cleanup. Track optional scenes, collectibles, combat challenges, and character progression so the final stretch does not become guesswork.

Quick Answer

For a 100 percent route, finish the story naturally while recording optional scenes, side areas, bosses, journals, music records, and character-specific combat tasks. Do not rush into a final cleanup without a written checklist.

Best Route

  1. Play the story normally and avoid skipping camp scenes.
  2. Upgrade weapons and spend Lumina points as new systems open.
  3. Mark every optional boss, collectible, and unexplored side path.
  4. Save endgame cleanup for after your party has stronger builds.
  5. Use a final checklist for hidden or low-completion achievements.

Achievements To Watch Early

Early unlocks such as breaking enemies, upgrading weapons, using Lumina points, and witnessing optional camp scenes are simple if you engage with each system. The harder part is remembering optional content that does not sit on the main route.

Achievement route placeholder

Cleanup Strategy

Leave boss perfection attempts and collectible sweeps until you understand the combat rhythm. A stronger party makes no-damage attempts and late optional fights less frustrating, while your notes prevent random backtracking.

FAQ

How many Steam achievements does Clair Obscur Expedition 33 have?

Steam lists 55 achievements for Clair Obscur Expedition 33.

Should I use a checklist from the start?

Yes. Even a simple note file is enough to track optional scenes, bosses, records, and journals.

Are combat achievements hard?

They are easier once you understand break timing, parries, and each character's core mechanic.

Should I chase every achievement before finishing the story?

No. Finish the story with good notes, then clean up the demanding tasks with stronger builds.

What is the biggest mistake?

Assuming you will remember every optional location later. Write it down when you see it.
