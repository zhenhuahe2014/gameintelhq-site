Arc Raiders Solo vs Co-op

Arc Raiders is easier to stabilize with a squad, but solo runs can still be rewarding if you enjoy cautious routes, cleaner decision-making, and higher pressure during extraction.

Batch 004 Published May 31, 2026
Cover image placeholder

Quick Answer

Choose solo if you want slower, tense loot runs where every decision is yours. Choose co-op if you want safer scouting, faster recovery, better combat coverage, and more consistent extraction attempts.

Solo Strengths

  • You keep full control over route choices and risk tolerance.
  • You can disengage quickly without coordinating a team retreat.
  • Loot decisions are simpler because there is no split priority.
  • Quiet play rewards patience, sound awareness, and map knowledge.

Co-op Strengths

  • Squads can watch more angles during looting and extraction.
  • Team roles make dangerous fights and recovery easier.
  • Communication helps identify threats before they collapse a run.
  • Shared planning can make repeated routes more efficient.
Extraction route placeholder

Best Recommendation

Start in co-op if you are learning extraction flow for the first time. Move into solo runs once you understand common danger zones, retreat paths, and when a small loot win is better than forcing a full route.

FAQ

Is Arc Raiders good solo?

Yes, if you like deliberate play and accept that mistakes are harder to recover from alone.

Is co-op better for beginners?

Usually. A group can share information and reduce the pressure of every single mistake.

Should solo players avoid fights?

Not always, but solo players should choose fights that have clear exit routes.

What is the biggest co-op advantage?

Information. A team can scan, cover, loot, and retreat with more awareness than one player.
