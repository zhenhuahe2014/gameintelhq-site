Arc Raiders Best Settings

The best Arc Raiders settings should make enemy movement, loot decisions, sound cues, and extraction fights easier to read under pressure.

Batch 005 Published May 31, 2026
Cover image placeholder

Quick Answer

Prioritize stable frame rate, low visual smear, readable shadows, clear audio, and a sensitivity you can control while tracking targets. Extraction games punish confusion more than imperfect graphics.

Display Settings

  • Choose performance stability over maximum visual quality.
  • Reduce motion blur if fast turns make targets hard to follow.
  • Keep brightness high enough to read interiors without washing out distance.
  • Avoid settings that add clutter around particles, reflections, or heavy effects.

Control And Audio Settings

  • Lower aim sensitivity until small corrections feel reliable.
  • Keep sprint, crouch, ping, interact, and reload easy to reach.
  • Use headphones or a clean stereo mix so footsteps and threats stand out.
  • Turn music down if it competes with extraction audio cues.

Best Recommendation

Run two low-risk matches after changing settings. If you notice enemies earlier, loot faster, and panic less during extraction, the setup is working.

FAQ

Should I use high graphics settings?

Only if performance stays stable. Clear reads beat prettier scenes during risky runs.

Is motion blur good?

Most competitive players should keep it low or off for cleaner target tracking.

What setting matters most?

Frame stability and sensitivity matter most because they affect every fight.

Should I change audio?

Yes. Lower competing audio until movement and threat cues are easy to identify.
