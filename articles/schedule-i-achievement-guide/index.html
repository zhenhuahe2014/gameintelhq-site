Schedule I Achievement Guide

Schedule I achievement progress is easiest when your operation is stable. Build clean money flow, keep inventory predictable, and delay risky expansion until you can support it.

Quick Answer

Do not chase every Schedule I achievement at once. First stabilize income and supply, then target business milestones, customer goals, property upgrades, and late cleanup tasks in separate sessions.

Best Achievement Order

  1. Complete basic tutorial and early sale milestones.
  2. Build reliable production before expanding locations.
  3. Track customer, cash, and product goals as separate lists.
  4. Upgrade only when the current loop runs without shortages.
  5. Use late-game saves for cleanup and riskier unlocks.

Early Game Focus

Your first achievement route should be boring in a good way: predictable stock, steady cash, and no rushed expansion. Consistency unlocks more progress than chaotic high-risk growth.

Progress checklist placeholder

Cleanup Advice

When you are ready to clean up achievements, group them by system. Do money goals together, customer goals together, and property or upgrade goals together. This keeps your operation from pulling in five directions at once.

FAQ

Should I expand fast for achievements?

No. Expand when your current setup can support the extra demand.

What should I track?

Track cash milestones, customer progress, product goals, property upgrades, and any unusual one-off unlocks.

Can I clean up achievements late?

Yes. A stable late-game operation is usually better for cleanup than a rushed early save.

What causes achievement runs to stall?

Poor inventory planning, scattered goals, and expanding before the money loop is dependable.

What is the safest approach?

Keep one main save for steady progression and only experiment when you can recover.
